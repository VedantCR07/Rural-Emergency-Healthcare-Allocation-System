# Rural Emergency Healthcare Allocation System

Rural regions within a radius of 10 km around Waknaghat often face challenges in accessing timely healthcare due to limited hospital capacity and significant distances from major medical centers. Managing emergency cases efficiently becomes difficult when beds, doctors, and resources are constrained. In such semi-rural environments, logical prioritization and allocation of patients based on proximity and severity become critical to preventing delays in treatment.

Our application, *Rural Healthcare Allocation System*, addresses this gap through:

• 📖 Patient registration

• 🏥 Hospital capacity tracking

• 🚨 Emergency case prioritization based on severity

• 🩺 Allocation of patients to suitable hospitals

• 📂 Persistent file storage for hospitals and patient records

The application, built in C++, is designed as a decision-support tool and does not replace professional medical judgment. AI tools are used responsibly, solely for code refinement, ensuring the ethical use of technology. The project highlights the practical application of object-oriented programming concepts in modeling real-world healthcare resource allocation scenarios.
